Twin primes

A twin prime is a pair of prime numbers with the smaller prime separated by 2 from the larger. The first twin primes are (3,5), (5,7), and (11,13). It is still not known whether there are infinitely many twin primes. This question is one of the great unsolved problems of mathematics.

The question of whether there are infinitely many twin primes is equivalent to the question of whether the system of equations
φ (n) = n-1
φ (n+2) = n+1
has infinitely many solutions.
