Distribution of prime numbers on the number line

Euclid's theorem proves that there are infinitely many prime numbers. Their properties have been studied by mathematicians for thousands of years and still no one has discovered whether there is a pattern to the way in which the primes are distributed among the natural numbers - let alone to define such a distribution.

This dialog shows the natural numbers >= 2. Every prime number is a pale blue dot; composite numbers are black dots. The size of the indicated range of natural numbers may be adjusted using the "+" and "-" controls. The "arrow" buttons at the top of the screen may be used to adjust the number line. If you move the mouse cursor over a number, the following information is displayed for that number:

  • The next smaller and next larger prime numbers
  • The actual number of primes in the range indicated and the number of prime numbers according to the prime number theorem of Gauss
  • The prime factorization of the number
  • The calculation of Goldbach's conjecture
  • The next larger and next smaller twin primes
  • Information about Prime Quadruplets and Sixtuplet Primes.
