Counting the number of prime numbers

In 300 BC, the Greek mathematician Euclid proved that there are infinitely many prime numbers. Carl Friedrich Gauss was the first to try to estimate the number of prime numbers up to a given number n. With this dialog, you can evaluate three different functions that count the prime numbers in a given range. Each of the three functions can be started, stopped and continued independently of the others.
  • The function π(n) gives the exact number of prime numbers, that are smaller than or equal to n. The value π(n) is calculated by counting the prime numbers.
The two other functions are approximations that are based on Gauss:
  • At the age of 15, Gauss offered a first approximation for the number of prime numbers.
  • He later improved on this with the logarithmic integral.
