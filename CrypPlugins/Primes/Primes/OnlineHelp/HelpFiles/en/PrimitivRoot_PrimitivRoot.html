Primitive root of a prime number

In number theory a primitive root modulo m is an element of a prime residue class with the property, that each element of the residue class (except for 0) can be represented as a power of the primitive root.
For example, 6 is a primitive root of the prime residue class of 11, which contains the numbers from 0 to 10.

6 is a primitive root, since
60 mod 11 = 1
61 mod 11 = 6
62 mod 11 = 3
63 mod 11 = 7
64 mod 11 = 9
65 mod 11 = 10
66 mod 11 = 5
67 mod 11 = 8
68 mod 11 = 4
69 mod 11 = 2
