Miller-Rabin test

The Miller-Rabin test is the most often used primality test. It is based on Fermat's Little Theorem. A very clear explanation of the procedure can be found here (in German). Because of the nature of this test, mistakes are possible: The method may produce a result which is supposedly prime, but is in fact composite. Such numbers are called strong pseudoprimes. The opposite case, that the test recognizes a number as composite, which in reality is prime, doesn't happen.
