Miller-Rabin test

The Miller-Rabin primality test is the test that is most often used. It is based on Fermat's Little Theorem. A very clear explanation of the procedure can be found here. Because of the nature of this test, mistakes are possible; for example the method may produce a result which is supposedly prime, but is in fact composite. Such numbers are called strong pseudoprimes.
