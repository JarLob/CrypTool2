Sieve of Eratosthenes

The sieve of Eratosthenes is a method for finding all prime numbers up to a given number. To test if a number is prime, one determines all primes smaller than the number and checks, if it is a multiple of any of them. The method dates back on the greek mathematician Eratosthenes of Cyrene (c. 200 BC).

Method:
Take the list of all numbers 1 to n. Start with the number 2. Cross out all multiples of 2 (excluding 2 itself) on the list. Find the next number that is not crossed out. In this case, it's the 3. Cross out all multiples of 3 (excluding 3). Repeat this with all number that are not yet crossed out, until you reach √n.
